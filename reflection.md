# Planning for research labs, open source contributions and internships

### Activity description

For the second **Choose Your Own Adventure** assignment I focused on **preparing** to join a research lab, contribute to open source and secure a summer 2027 software engineering internship.  Because the spring semester has just ended, I have not yet taken concrete actions; instead, I created a preparation plan informed by articles, guides and my career goals.  I researched how undergraduates can get involved in faculty‑led research, identified strategies for discovering open source projects and planned steps for a competitive internship search.  The planning process involved reading official resources and compiling notes into a clear timeline.

### Key decisions

Several key decisions shaped this plan.  First, I chose to prioritise joining a research lab because universities actively support undergraduate researchers regardless of major, and faculty members value curiosity and dedication over perfect grades.  I decided to focus on labs related to cybersecurity, networking or data science since these areas align with my interests.  Second, I planned to contribute to an open source project.  Open source participation offers many benefits that improve skills, meeting mentors, building a public reputation and practising leadership and contributions can start small with documentation or typo fixes.  Third, I recognized that landing a software engineering internship requires early preparation, self‑assessment and persistence.  I therefore scheduled time this summer to develop side projects, practise technical interviews and network with industry professionals.

### Contributions

This was an individual planning exercise, but I drew on multiple external sources.  I compiled research notes summarising how to identify potential labs, approach professors and leverage departmental resources.  I reviewed the Open Source Guides to learn about evaluating projects, finding beginner‑friendly issues and communicating effectively in communities.  For internship preparation, I relied on CodePath’s guidance that emphasises starting the search early, applying widely and building projects.  These sources helped me create a structured plan documented in `planning_notes.md`.

### Quality assessment

I am satisfied with the level of detail and organisation in my preparation plan.  Breaking down each goal into actionable steps and identifying labs, attending seminars, drafting outreach emails, evaluating open source projects and scheduling interview practice which provides clarity on what I need to do and when.  My research confirmed that motivation and curiosity matter more than perfect grades for lab positions, which gives me confidence as I reach out to professors.  Including resources like GitHub Explore and First Timers Only for open source ensures that I can find beginner‑friendly issues.  I also recognised the importance of starting internship applications early and building a strong portfolio.  If I were to improve the plan, I would set more specific milestones. For example, commit to reaching out to two professors by August 1 and submit at least one pull request by September.  I could also seek feedback from career advisors or peers to refine my outreach emails and project ideas.

### Career alignment

This planning activity aligns well with my short‑ and long‑term career goals.  Working in a research lab will deepen my technical expertise and expose me to the investigative process; open source contributions will hone my collaboration skills and build a public track record.  Both experiences will strengthen my candidacy for internships and future employment.  Preparing early for internship applications—including self assessment, networking and portfolio building which sets me up for success in summer 2027.  Ultimately, this plan moves me closer to a career in cybersecurity or research and development by equipping me with the skills, relationships and experiences that employers and graduate programs value.  Even though I have not yet started these activities, the thoughtful planning documented here positions me to act strategically and effectively when I begin.
