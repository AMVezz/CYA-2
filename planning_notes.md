# Preparation plan for CYA‑2

This document outlines the tasks I will initiate during summer 2026 to prepare for joining a research lab at WSU, contributing to open source and securing a software engineering internship for summer 2027.  These steps are drawn from research‑backed recommendations and personal career goals.

## 1 Join a research lab at WSU

- **Identify potential labs:** Explore WSU department websites and faculty profiles to find research that aligns with my interests (e.g., cybersecurity, networking, data science).  Professors usually list their research topics, current projects and publications on departmental pages.  I will make a list of 2–3 labs to contact.
- **Attend seminars and events:** Attend department seminars, poster sessions and undergraduate research symposia to learn more about ongoing research and meet faculty and graduate students.
- **Network with TAs and upperclassmen:** Graduate teaching assistants and upperclassmen already involved in research can provide insights into lab culture and suggest opportunities.
- **Prepare outreach:** Draft a concise, personalized email for each professor.  The message will introduce myself, explain why I’m interested in their research, mention relevant coursework and highlight my availability.
- **Develop foundational knowledge:** Review relevant papers and brush up on prerequisite topics (e.g., network security, machine learning) so I can contribute meaningfully.  Because prior research experience is not required and motivation matters more than perfect grades, I’ll focus on demonstrating curiosity and commitment.

## 2 Contribute to an open source project

- **Choose a project I use:** Start by thinking about software I rely on or find interesting; contributors often begin by improving tools they already use.  When I notice a bug or documentation issue, I will consider fixing it.
- **Look for beginner‑friendly issues:** Use the `/contribute` page on GitHub repositories and resources such as GitHub Explore, First Timers Only and Up For Grabs to find issues suited for new contributors.
- **Focus on documentation and small fixes:** Open source contributions don’t have to be code.  Documentation, design and organization tasks are often neglected.  I will start by fixing typos, improving README instructions or answering questions.
- **Evaluate project health:** Before contributing, check whether the project has an open source license, active commits, responsive maintainers and welcoming community.
- **Practice collaboration:** When submitting issues or pull requests, provide context, do my homework and keep messages concise.

## 3 Prepare for a software engineering internship

- **Start early and apply widely:** Begin searching for internship opportunities by early fall 2026.  It’s common to apply to many companies and hear back from only a few, so persistence is key.
- **Self‑assess skills:** Reflect on my strengths and weaknesses, particularly programming languages and coursework.  Align internship targets with areas where I excel and want to grow.
- **List target companies:** Make a list of companies whose products I admire and search for internship roles with them.  Keep track of application deadlines and requirements.
- **Build side projects:** Develop personal projects to showcase my abilities.  Projects could include web applications, mobile apps or contributions to open source, and they will live on my GitHub profile to act as a living résumé.
- **Strengthen interview skills:** Practice algorithms and data structures ahead of technical interviews.  Use online resources or join preparation courses.
- **Network:** Attend career fairs, join student groups and connect with industry professionals.  Strong networks are critical for finding internships and job leads.

## Timeline

- **May: June 2026:** Research WSU labs, compile a list of potential faculty mentors and attend any available seminars.  Identify open source projects of interest and start exploring their contribution guidelines.
- **July: August 2026:** Draft outreach emails to professors, connect with graduate students or TAs, and work on small open source contributions (documentation, typos, etc.).  Begin early research on internship postings and prepare a résumé for the application season.
- **September: December 2026:** Send emails to labs, meet potential mentors and hopefully join a lab.  Continue contributing to open source and develop side projects.  Apply to internship programs and practice technical interviews.
